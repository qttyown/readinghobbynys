MR PIRATE

by CIK ZALIKHA

☆ ☆ ☆ ☆ ☆

🧚‍♀️ SYNOPSIS 🧚‍♀️

"Siapalah engkau agaknya, Mr Blue?" Beberapa tahun lalu, seorang lelaki bermata biru menyelamatkan nyawa Husna. Sejak itu, wajahnya sering muncul dalam mimpi. Mata biru itu sangat memukau. Buatkan dia jatuh cinta pada pandangan kali pertama mata mereka bertembung.

Kemunculan Nathan, bakal CEO Ocean Resort yang juga bermata biru, buat Husna yakin dialah Mr Blue. Namun, sebelum sempat meluahkan perasaan, Husna terperangkap di sebuah pulau yang merupakan kawasan zon merah yang dikawal oleh seorang Lanun kejam. Lanun itu tak berperikemanusiaan, mulutnya kasar, tapi ada sesuatu dalam dirinya yang buat Husna keliru. Adakah dia hanya tawanan... atau lebih dari itu?

Nathan nekad mahu menyelamatkannya. Tapi Husna? Dia mahu cari jalan keluar sendiri. Siapakah Mr Blue sebenarnya? "It's you?!" – Husna

🌻 MY REVIEW 🌻

To me, this is one of the most unique stories I’ve ever read so far, because the author highlights Dusun language and Sabahan islands, something I’ve never come across before in other stories. The male character, known as Lanun, isn’t actually a bad pirate. The villagers call him that because he protects them from real pirates. His real name is Andika Rafhael, a rich man who chose to settle on his late mother’s village island in Sabah. The female lead, Husna, is a successful and slightly arrogant woman. She has an evil stepmother who once tried to kill her by pushing her into the sea. That’s when Lanun saves her from drowning. Husna and Lanun have a love-hate, cat-and-dog type of relationship, always arguing. But as the story goes on, they start to care for each other. However, Husna’s father disapproves of their relationship because he believes Lanun is just a poor villager. Despite all the challenges, they end up together in the end.

I rate this story 8.5/10! I think the storyline follows a typical Malay romance structure, but what makes it different is the character Lanun himself. I’m so impressed by the author because each chapter includes phrases in the Dusun language. It felt like I was learning about our local culture while reading. For example, "Koupusan" means "dearest" and "Pounsikou" means "thank you".
